Hello, I'm

Hunter Langemo

and I would love to serve
you as your SGA President!

What does it look like to give the people what they want?

As your current SGA Treasurer:

  • Increased Club Funding - Increased budget cap from $1500/year to $2000/semester and increased club subsidy to $100/semester to $250/semester. Simplified Budget form to streamline process for club officers upon popular demand.
  • Gym hours extended to midnight - Fulfilled campaign promise and addressed the concern of plurality of students in SGA Fitness Center Survey. Additional hours of operation ranked as students' top improvement for the Fitness Center and more than 50% opted for hours 10-12PM (742 Responses).
  • Campus Improvements - Installation of 7 new EZH20 water fillers. Students in T2, Haas Commons, Army ROTC, Air Force ROTC, STEM, and the DLC now have access to filtered water as drinking fountains are not it use.

Founding the SGA Student Treasury:

Created a board revolved around innovating the student experience. This board researches various universities and collaborates to bring the best ideas and services back to the ERAU Prescott campus. We surveyed the student body to discover what the people want. The 300+ responses determined my agenda for the Spring.  

What did the people say?

  • Residence Hall Printers - Since a majority of students expressed support, I have worked with the Director of Housing and Residence Life and the Assistant Director of Information Technology on this project. The printers are on-site, and we are currently finishing up the rest of the logistics.
  • Recycling/Compost Program - Also garnishing a majority of student support, I have collaborated with RC^2, Prescott College, Alternative Energy Association, the Sustainability Committee Chair, the Regional VP of Sodexo, the director of Facilities, and many other administrators to bring a program to ERAU Prescott.
  • Recreational Equipment Service - Provides students with an opportunity to check out free kayaks, paddle boards, hiking, fishing, and other recreational equipment for FREE. Worked with the Assistant Director of Athletics, Intramural Coordinator and the SGA Council to make this possible.
  • Discount Card  - Collaboration with local businesses to provide students discounts in the community.
  • Skate Park - Looked into the feasibility of putting a skate park on campus, similar to the size and complexity of the recent SGA-funded basketball court. Reached out to Facilities and Risk Management to look into the logistics.

Presidential Outreach:

As your President, I would represent 3000 students. I cannot just run off of the ideas of one student.

  • External Dropbox of Ideas - For students that do not want to come into the office or do not have time, I would place a drop box outside where students can anonymously leave feedback on administration, SGA, or any other concerns they face as a student.
  • Surveys - As I polled students via the Student Treasury survey, I would continue to survey students concerning Student Union considerations, Board of Trustees reports, and any other pivotal decisions directly impacting the student body. 
  • Weekly Outreach - I have spent the last several weeks talking to hundreds of different students, gathering as much feedback as possible. With over eight pages and counting of feedback, I would continue to reach out to students on a weekly basis as President.

What else did the people say:

  • Mental Health Awareness - This past year has negatively impacted so many individuals, and mental health has plummeted. 35% of college students suffered with a mental illness pre-pandemic. I lost a loved one at a young age to suicide, so I do not take this lightly. We must increase awareness, useful resources, and implement campus-wide programs to help address this issue. But at the root of the problem, we have to give students more academic breaks.
  • Title IX Concerns - Of all issues, more students have brought forth discrepancies regarding Title IX on campus. Due to this vital concern, I have taken action by meeting weekly with the Title IX Coordinator and Investigator, as well as my Daytona counterpart. I will hold administration accountable, work to provide better education of resources and processes, hold more effective trainings, and review University Sexual Misconduct and Title IX policies to reform any necessary change in the jurisdiction in the university. Above all, I will work upper administration to separate the Assistant Dean of Students and Title IX Coordinator roles so the Title IX Coordinator could dedicate 100% of their time and effort into Title IX, as Daytona’s Coordinator gets to do.
  • Administration Transparency - So many students have brought forth concerns regarding transparency. With my experience working with administrators, I strive to foster unity, clarity, and reestablish trust between students and administration, without sacrificing student advocation. I also plan to work with the Director of Housing and Residence Life to bring an SGA HRL representative to council to bring a direct line of communication between the 1400 students living on campus and HRL administration.
